Three Important Data Roles

July 2023 | by Loni Tande

The threee important data roles are:

  1. Data Science
  2. Data Egineering
  3. Data Analytics
  4. NLP Engineer

Data science refers to the field of extracting insights and knowledge from data using various statistical and computational
techniques. Data scientists are responsible for developing algorithms, models, and statistical analyses to extract insights
and actionable information from complex datasets.

Data engineering is all about the design, construction, maintenance, and scaling of the data architecture and infrastructure
that supports business applications and analytics. Data engineers build and manage the data pipelines that allow data
scientists and analysts to extract insights from the data.

Data analytics is the process of examining and interpreting data to discern patterns and draw conclusions from the data.
Data analysts analyze large amounts of information and communicate their findings to non-technical stakeholders in a
clear and concise way, driving data-driven decision-making in the business.
They also collaborate with data scientists and engineers to ensure the data is clean, reliable and easy to access.

NLP stands for Natural Language Processing. These engineers usually find themselves working with textual data.Some of their activities include Sentiment analysis , building language and translation models and somtimes they prepare training datasets.

In conclusion, the field of data science, data engineering, and data analytics are all integral components of data-driven
decision-making in the modern business landscape.While data scientists develop algorithms and models to extract insights
and knowledge from data, data engineers ensure the underlying data architecture and infrastructure is reliable and scalable.
Data analysts, on the other hand, translate the findings into easy to understand results which help non-technical stakeholders
make informed decisions.Together, these roles play a critical role in driving innovation, increasing efficiency, and creating
a competitive edge for businesses across all industries.
